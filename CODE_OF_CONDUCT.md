# Code of Conduct
Liquidity Sequencing Lab (LSI)

This project maintains a high standard of professionalism, academic rigor, and technical precision.  
All contributors, collaborators, and reviewers must adhere to the following code of conduct.

---

## 1. Professionalism
- Treat all contributors with respect.  
- Debate ideas rigorously, never people.  
- No hostile, inflammatory, or disruptive behavior.

---

## 2. Academic Integrity
- No plagiarism of any kind.  
- All external ideas, models, or text must be cited.  
- No fabrication or manipulation of research results.  
- Diagrams and figures must represent accurate information.

---

## 3. Technical Integrity
- Models must be mathematically sound.  
- Simulations must be reproducible.  
- Architectural documentation must be coherent and accurate.  
- No misleading claims or unverifiable assertions.

---

## 4. Communication Standards
- Write clearly, precisely, and concisely.  
- Avoid ambiguity in terminology.  
- Document assumptions explicitly.  
- Provide context for all contributions.

---

## 5. Collaboration Rules
- All pull requests must follow `CONTRIBUTING.md`.  
- All discussions must stay focused on improving LSI.  
- No off-topic, irrelevant, or low-quality contributions.

---

## 6. Unacceptable Behavior
- Personal attacks  
- Dismissive or derogatory comments  
- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusion  
- Posting sensitive or confidential information  
- Submitting intentionally low-quality or malicious content  

Such actions may result in removal from the contributor list and revocation of contribution privileges.

---

## 7. Enforcement
Violations of this code will be reviewed by the maintainer (Henry Maloba).  
Consequences may include:
- Request for revisions  
- Rejection of contributions  
- Temporary or permanent ban from contributing  

All enforcement decisions are final.

---

## 8. Reporting Issues
Concerns or violations can be reported via:
- GitHub Issues (technical concerns)  
- Direct communication with the maintainer (conduct concerns)  

Reports must include:
- A clear description of the issue  
- Evidence (if relevant)  
- Impact on the project

---

## 9. Commitment
By participating in this project, you agree to uphold these standards and contribute to a professional, rigorous, and respectful research environment.
